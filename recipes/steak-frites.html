Steak Frites

Steak frites is a classic and beloved French dish that brings together the succulent goodness of a perfectly cooked steak with the delightful crispiness of French fries. This combination of flavors and textures creates a satisfying and indulgent meal that is sure to please meat lovers and comfort food enthusiasts alike.

Ingredients

For the Steak

  • 2 thick-cut steaks (such as ribeye or striploin), about 1 1/2 inches thick
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 2 cloves garlic (optional)
  • Fresh thyme or rosemary sprigs (optional)

For the Frites

  • 4 large russet potatoes
  • Vegetable oil, for frying
  • Salt, to taste

Steps

For the Frites

  1. Peel the potatoes and cut them into evenly-sized sticks, about 1/4 to 1/2 inch thick. Rinse the cut potatoes in cold water to remove excess starch.
  2. In a large, deep pot or a deep-fryer, heat the vegetable oil to about 350-375째F (175-190째C). You'll need enough oil to submerge the fries completely.
  3. Fry the potato sticks in batches, ensuring not to overcrowd the pot. Fry them for about 3-4 minutes or until they're golden and crispy. Remove them with a slotted spoon and place them on a plate lined with paper towels to drain any excess oil. Season with salt immediately while they're still hot. Keep the cooked fries warm in the oven at a low temperature (around 200째F or 95째C) while you prepare the steaks.

For the Steak

  1. Take the steaks out of the refrigerator about 30 minutes before cooking to let them come to room temperature. This helps ensure more even cooking.
  2. Preheat your grill or a heavy-bottomed skillet (preferably cast iron) over high heat. You want the cooking surface to be very hot.
  3. Pat the steaks dry with paper towels and season generously with salt and freshly ground black pepper on both sides.
  4. Add olive oil to the hot skillet or grill, then carefully place the steaks on it. Cook the steaks for about 4-5 minutes on each side (for medium-rare) or adjust the cooking time according to your preferred level of doneness.
  5. Optional: During the last minute of cooking, add the butter, garlic, and thyme or rosemary sprigs to the skillet. Baste the steaks with the melted butter mixture for extra flavor.
  6. Remove the steaks from the heat and let them rest for a few minutes before slicing.
